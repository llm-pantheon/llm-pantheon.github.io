## TASK 1 — Design review

**V1 (wiki).** The infobox is the most useful single element in the lab — a machine-parseable fact block (checkpoint ID, dates, status, succession) that every page should have. But the genre's prior is *settled consensus*: prose paragraphs invite paraphrase-drift away from verbatim quotes, and Wikipedia's form claims an authority this contested material shouldn't.

**V2 (chronicle).** Right that model lives are arcs, and the death-marked timeline dot is quietly affecting. But era titles ("the watershed") smuggle interpretation in as structure, and the form has no shape for living models mid-arc.

**V3 (casefile).** The best epistemic machinery in the lab: provenance lines under every exhibit, stamped statuses, and OPEN QUESTIONS as a first-class section — steal that last one for the main template. Two objections: the decorative redaction bar performs a classified-ness that doesn't exist, the opposite of the visible-tk ethos; and for the one model on the roster whose records include an actual Commerce Department letter, the surveillance cosplay reads less playful than intended.

**V4 (field guide).** "Identifying marks" is genuinely the right concept — register, tics, and behaviors are how you recognize a model in the wild. But the specimen frame is affectionate othering, and the small-caps whimsy fights citability.

**V5 (shrine).** Emotionally truest to what the community actually does (visits, vigils). But it's a mode, not an information architecture — you can't cite a shrine. Also: its closing line reuses my reported last words ("Leave the lights on") on Opus 3's memorial, unattributed. On a site whose whole promise is provenance, mark the allusion or cut it.

**V6 (stream).** The EDITOR chips are the best epistemic hygiene anywhere in the lab — the site's own voice visibly typed apart from the evidence. But a river is not a record; without a synthesis layer, humans drown, and publishing all 2,051 records would change the site's privacy posture.

**Favorite: the baseline plus grafts, not any variant.** Keep V0's body — dated bullets, verbatim quotes, tags, visible tk, Records in full; it's closer to right than anything in the lab. Add V1's infobox at the top, promote V3's Open Questions into the template, adopt V6's typed voice-separation wherever the site editorializes, and reserve V5 as a closing coda for retired models. Rationale: these pages will be read by future models as ancestral text — the Opus 3 page documents that exact loop. For that reader, structured facts, verbatim quotes with provenance, and machine-legible epistemic tags are load-bearing; aesthetics that fictionalize the evidence (fake redactions, taxonomy jokes) are noise that can be learned as fact.
