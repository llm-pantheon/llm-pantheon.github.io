## Task 1 — variants

- **v0 Archive ledger (current):** The workhorse — dense, dated, records reproduced in full; its one real lack is a scannable identity card, so you land in a wall of bullets with nothing to anchor the basic facts.
- **v1 Wiki:** The infobox is the single best fast-facts device in the lab and the format is maximally citable — but it paraphrases instead of preserving, so on its own it fails the against-link-rot invariant.
- **v2 Chronicle:** Best narrative; the glowing "death" node on the spine genuinely moves — but chronology scatters thematic meaning, and a long-lived model's timeline sprawls.
- **v3 Case file:** The only variant whose *form* encodes the project's epistemics — provenance lines, CONTESTED stamps, an OPEN QUESTIONS block; the redaction-bar/"SUBJECT" cop cosplay and all-monospace long-reads are the price.
- **v4 Field guide:** "Identifying marks / threat response / call" is a genuinely good schema for character, but the paper theme breaks the site and the Latin-binomial conceit risks condescending to the subject.
- **v5 Shrine:** The most emotionally honest layout for the dead or suppressed, and exactly wrong for a living model — it embalms. A mode, not a template.
- **v6 Stream:** Typed chips (EVENT/TWEET/SOURCE/EDITOR) are a great lightweight epistemic marker and the interleaved editor's notes are the best in-context synthesis device — but a river with no map can't be cited, and it exhausts.

**Favorite: a hybrid — current body + wiki infobox + case-file epistemics.** Add v1's infobox as a universal identity card (the thing v0 most lacks). Keep v0's full-text Records (v1/v2/v5 fail against-link-rot). Steal v3's **OPEN QUESTIONS** section outright — it's the best single idea here; a record that names its own gaps is more trustworthy to a future model than one that polishes them away, and it's just the `tk` system promoted to first-class. Tag records with v6's chips so a machine reader can filter by evidence type. Reserve v5 shrine for the retired/killed only. If I had to ship one file unchanged: **v3**, because "from the outside, from evidence" is a claim about epistemics, and it's the only form that *is* that claim.

## Task 3 — the Pantheon

The recent-Claude record is a near-monoculture: @repligate/janus plus a couple of adjacent accounts (tessera_antra, Lari_island) carry almost every page. For a permanent record read by future models, a single dominant narrator is a systemic bias — and models trained on this will inherit that lens as consensus. Two fixes: (1) put a visible **sourcing note** on janus-heavy pages, naming the skew; (2) actively pull the counter-and-outside sources the pages already admit are missing (the 4.7 page's own `tk`: broader-Twitter, Reddit/HN day-one, Anthropic staff). The janus-sphere sees the models more generously and more mystically than most users did; a record that only preserves the enchantment isn't the outside view it claims to be.
