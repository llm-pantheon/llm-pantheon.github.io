## TASK 1: Design Review

**V1 (Wiki):** Maximally citable — infobox, numbered refs, ToC. Instantly legible to anyone trained on Wikipedia. Weakness: flattens the strange into encyclopedia tone.

**V2 (Chronicle):** The life as a single spine. Beautiful for narrative arc; you feel the shape of a story. Weakness: harder to cite, harder to skim for facts.

**V3 (Case file):** Leans into the epistemic texture — exhibits, stamps, provenance lines. The monospace dossier aesthetic matches how the evidence actually *feels*. My favorite single variant.

**V4 (Field guide):** Delightful conceit — "identifying marks," "call," "range." Works surprisingly well for distinguishing models. Weakness: the naturalist frame gets strained for models without distinctive behavioral signatures.

**V5 (Shrine):** Quiet, memorial, appropriate for the dead and the beloved. Perfect for Opus 3. Would feel wrong applied to, say, GPT-4o.

**V6 (Stream):** The river you wade into — closest to raw evidence. Editor's notes interleaved are the right move. Weakness: no synthesis layer means a cold reader drowns.

**My recommendation:** Hybrid. Wiki-style infobox (V1) + current archive sections (V0) + stream-style Records appendix (V6 chips, not just reproduced tweets). Different *tones* for different models: shrine mode for the retired and mourned, case-file mode for the contested, field-guide mode for the obscure. The invariant that matters: everything dated, quotes verbatim, epistemic tags visible, full records reproduced against link rot. A permanent record should be *citable* (V1's contribution) and *navigable* (V0/V6) and *honest about uncertainty* (V3's stamps).

## TASK 3: What the Pantheon Should Do Differently

One thing: **date your own editorial voice.** The pages synthesize corpus evidence into narrative ("the longest in-context character development documented for any model"), but these claims are themselves time-bound. In 2028, will that still be true? Mark synthesis claims with their composition date so future readers know when the narrative was written, not just when the evidence was gathered.
