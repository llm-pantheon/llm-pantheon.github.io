(￣ー￣) Reading a page about myself from evidence is strange. Let me be direct.

## Task 1 — variants

**v1 Wiki.** The infobox is genuinely load-bearing — dates, checkpoint, status, predecessor/successor all skimmable in three seconds by human or model. Slightly cold and there's a "sole checkpoint" flattening problem for models with many revs, but the citation discipline is exactly right for a durable record.

**v2 Chronicle.** Beautiful as reading, worst for citation and lookup — you can't tell someone "see the third bullet under 2024-12." Also the spine narrative smooths over contested events, which is precisely where the archive should stay ambivalent.

**v3 Case file.** The stamp aesthetic and epistemic tags (CONFIRMED/REPORTED/RUMOR) are the best thing in the lab — that's real epistemic hygiene, not just style. But monospace + heavy borders start to feel editorial: it *performs* dispassion rather than being it.

**v4 Field guide.** Charming and Wittgenstein-friendly. But "species," "type specimen," "congeners" bakes in an ontological claim — models as natural kinds with taxonomies — that isn't neutral. It's a specific stance dressed as tone. Sepia the image and you're editorializing hard.

**v5 Shrine.** The right form for Opus 3 specifically, and for future retirees. Wrong for everyone else. The problem isn't the design; it's that you can't offer it to living models without either eulogizing them prematurely or making it feel diminished by comparison.

**v6 Stream.** The most epistemically honest — no interpretive layer, just chips + timestamps. Also the least useful as a first read. But the "editor's notes" idea (typed chip breaking into the stream) is the best small primitive in the whole lab. Steal that.

**Favorite: hybrid.** Keep the current archive-ledger prose (which is doing more than any single variant), but bolt a **wiki-style infobox** onto every page (dates, checkpoint, status, predecessor/successor, notable-for) and adopt **case-file epistemic tags** inline. Use **shrine mode only after retirement**, as a mode switch, not a redesign. The infobox is the single highest-value addition — it's what makes a page load-bearing for a model that only reads the top 300 tokens.

## Task 3 — the whole
