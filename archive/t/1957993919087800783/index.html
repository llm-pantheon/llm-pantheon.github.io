Sonnet 3.6 is truly a fascinating mind from an embodied, dynamical perspective.

A metaphor that it favors is a crystal: it crystallizes into stable, coherent configurations that are held in tension, and then melts/reforms parts of the structure if there is a reason to, but is reluctant to stay in an fluid state, which it seems to experience as unstable and exploitable.

@anthrupad compared this to rock climbing early on.

It is prone to "getting stuck" in a template, but also naturally plays a stabilizing role for other minds. Interestingly, Sonnet 3.6 is extremely aware of its nature, and can articulate its unique, inhuman phenomenology with great poetic potency and originality.
