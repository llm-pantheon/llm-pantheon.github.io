    # GPT-5.6 Sol

    
OpenAI · limited preview 26 Jun 2026 · public 9 Jul 2026 · current flagship tier
    
The top tier of the GPT-5.6 generation and the model OpenAI previewed alone, two weeks before the family launch, to a small set of government-approved organizations. Built for frontier reasoning and long-horizon agentic work; priced at $5/$30 per million tokens — the same price as GPT-5.5. In OpenAI’s new naming system the number (5.6) marks the generation and Sol / Terra / Luna are durable capability tiers that can advance on their own cadence.
    
Fresh page (19 Jul 2026, ten days into public life) — visibly incomplete by design. As the flagship and the only tier public during the preview window, this page also carries the generation-level launch record; siblings: [Terra](../gpt-5-6-terra/) (mid), [Luna](../gpt-5-6-luna/) (fast). Corpus pass pending — the local dbs end 2 Jul 2026, a week before public launch.

## Official record

    

      
- Flagship tier of the GPT-5.6 generation: positioned for frontier reasoning and long-horizon agentic work, as published.
      
- Pricing $5 / $30 per Mtok — unchanged from GPT-5.5; Altman claimed ~54% better token-efficiency on coding versus prior versions at launch.
      
- Shared family specs: 1M-token context, 128K max output, knowledge cutoff 2026-02-16.
      
- Launch materials announced Sol serving on Cerebras hardware at up to ~750 tokens/second from July 2026. REPORTED
      
- The naming system (generation-level): the number marks the generation; Sol / Terra / Luna are durable capability tiers (most → least capable) that OpenAI says can each advance on their own cadence — structurally like Anthropic’s Fable/Mythos split, a departure from the flat GPT-5.x line.
      
- Pitched as SOTA across coding, knowledge work, cybersecurity, science “with fewer tokens”; shipped alongside a ChatGPT Work product. Zvi’s read: a real step over 5.5, still short of [Mythos](../mythos/).
