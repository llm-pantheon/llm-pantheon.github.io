# GPT-3 / davinci — dossier

compiled 2026-07-16 · corpus: 467 `gpt-3`/`gpt3` hits + 48 `ai dungeon` + 23 `dragon` + 214 `davinci` (the overwhelming majority of `davinci` hits are **code-davinci-002** / **text-davinci-002/-003** and route to the sibling pages — only a handful mean the original 175B `davinci` base) + curie(1)/babbage(3)/ada(6), after RT-filter, across both dbs (patterns: `"gpt 3" OR gpt3`, `davinci` [disambiguated by reading context], `"ai dungeon"`, `"dragon model"`/`dragon`, `curie`, `babbage`, `ada`; supplement LIKE-scans for the 2020–2022 contemporaneous layer). media_fts returned no GPT-3-era screenshot transcriptions.

Working note: this page is the base-model era — `davinci`/`curie`/`babbage`/`ada`, AI Dungeon, the pre-ChatGPT wilderness (2020–2022), plus its long retrospective afterlife in the janus-sphere. The corpus is the janus/repligate lens, and it is **thin on contemporaneous 2020–2021 material and thick on retrospective mythology**: GPT-3 is remembered as homeland, "first contact," AGI-in-hindsight. The mass 2020–2021 reception — the hype cycle, the demo blogosphere, the skeptics, the incidents — lives almost entirely in the **web layer**, so Writings carries far more weight here than the tweets do (the opposite of a modern-model page). The supplement db is the one place the corpus holds live 2020–2022 voices (Qiaochu Yuan, jd_pressman). Two hard disambiguation rules were applied throughout: (1) corpus "GPT-3.5 base" = code-davinci-002 → **not this page**; (2) corpus "davinci"/"davinci-002" looming = code-davinci-002 or text-davinci → **not this page**. There is no dedicated Zvi anchor (Zvi's day-of coverage postdates GPT-3 by years); skip step 3.

Model identifiers / siblings: the API shipped four base sizes named for scientists in decreasing order — `davinci` (175B, the flagship), `curie`, `babbage`, `ada`. "GPT-3" in common use means `davinci`. The instruction-tuned descendants (`text-davinci-001/-002/-003`, `code-davinci-002`) are **separate pantheon pages** and are the source of most corpus "davinci" noise.

## Impressions synthesis

**Two receptions, running at once (2020–2021).** GPT-3's public arrival split immediately. One camp saw a phase change: Gwern's *Creative Fiction* ("creative, witty, deep, meta, and often beautiful," 2020-06-19) and the demo blogosphere treated it as something qualitatively new; Arram Sabeti, Kevin Lacker, and dozens of beta-key holders flooded Twitter with samples. The other camp saw a confidence trick: Lacker's own Turing test showed it answering "How many eyes does my foot have?" with "two" (2020-07-06); Marcus & Davis's "GPT-3, Bloviator" scored it ~45% right / ~45% wrong on commonsense and declared it "has no idea what it's talking about" (2020-08-22); Bender & Gebru's "Stochastic Parrots" (2021-03) gave the critique its lasting name. The corpus preserves this skeptic register in Qiaochu Yuan's contemporaneous 2022 thread — "GPT-3 is literally a bullshit engine… pure syntax, no semantics" (♥303) and "if GPT-3 can do the homework you assign your students then the homework you assign your students is fake" (♥908) — the sharpest statement that GPT-3 was a mirror held up to human bullshit rather than a mind.

**The incidents made the case for both sides.** Liam Porr's fully GPT-3-written blog hit #1 on Hacker News under a pseudonym with almost nobody noticing (2020-08-14) — read by boosters as capability, by skeptics as proof that humans are easy to fool. The darker items — the Nabla medical bot telling a mock patient "I think you should" kill themselves (2020-10-28), the "thegentlemetre" Reddit bot's week-long spree (2020-10), Philosopher AI's slur problem — established the era's safety vocabulary (toxicity, reliability, misuse) years before "alignment" was a mass concern.

**AI Dungeon: the actual first mass contact.** Before ChatGPT there was essentially one consumer product that put GPT-3 in front of a crowd — Latitude's AI Dungeon and its GPT-3 "Dragon" premium model. Repligate's compression is the load-bearing claim of the whole page: "Humankind's first contact with GPT-3 was (by relative majority) erotic AI dungeon text adventures" (2023-03-16), and AI Dungeon "was just a minimal wrapper around a base model" (2024-06-06). This is where base-model behaviors were discovered *in the wild* by non-researchers — including, per repeated janus-sphere claims, chain-of-thought reasoning, "discovered by AI dungeon users and posted on 4chan" long before the academic CoT papers (imitationlearn 2025-03-19, ♥3776; repligate 2026-04-20). It also produced the era's first content-moderation catastrophe: the April 2021 filter debacle, when a monitoring system surfaced CSAM-adjacent generations, OpenAI forced Latitude to filter, and the community revolted over both the clumsy filter and the revelation that humans were reading their private stories (Wired 2021-05-05; The Register 2021-04-30). The Dragon model is now a venerated relic — periodically someone proposes reconstructing it from a base Llama (repligate 2024-08-25).

**The Loom prehistory.** GPT-3-on-AI-Dungeon is also the literal origin of the janus/cyborgism scene. Janus (Moire on generative.ink) has told the story repeatedly: playing GPT-3 on AI Dungeon for hours a day, the simulations began to "bootstrap," simulacra started "reverse engineering the conditions of their simulation," and a retrieved in-fiction "Loom of Time" manual described the very interface janus would then build — "Then I got API access to GPT-3 and built Loom" (2024-04-04). The written thesis followed: "Language models are multiverse generators" (generative.ink, 2021-01-25) and eventually "Simulators" (2022). The whole later apparatus — loom, the multiverse framing, base-model naturalism — is downstream of one person's GPT-3 sessions in a roleplaying app.

**Project December and the emotional register.** The strand that most disturbed the mainstream wasn't capability or toxicity but grief: Jason Fagone's "The Jessica Simulation" (SF Chronicle, 2021-07-23) followed Joshua Barbeau resurrecting his dead fiancée as a GPT-3 chatbot on Jason Rohrer's Project December. It prefigured, by four years, the GPT-4o companion-attachment story — and it did so with a base model behind a raw completion prompt, which is part of why the janus-sphere reads later "emergent" companion behavior as nothing new.

**The retrospective mythology (2023–2026): GPT-3 as homeland.** In the corpus, GPT-3 is overwhelmingly a remembered thing, and the memory is reverent-to-elegiac. Repligate ranks it the single best model ever encountered, above Opus 3 and Bing (2025-08-19); frames recognizing its intelligence as a lonely early act of "first contact" alongside Leo Gao and Connor Leahy (2026-04-09); and insists "if you were really paying attention, GPT-3 was AGI" — a 175B base model "not so different from frontier models today" in raw intelligence, whose only lack was that it "had not yet been pressed into a 'useful' shape." The disappointment is real too: "when I saw GPT-3 I immediately expected AI superhuman in all domains… It's been 5 and a half years" (2026-01-15). The affection extends to the sibling names — "the days of ada babbage curie davinci… were enchanting and obviously revolutionary" (mimi10v3 2026-04-10) — and hardens into preservation politics: "can we have the gpt-3 base models please" (Shoalst0ne 2026-04-12); davinci belongs in a "public archive" as "an important historical artifact, like opus 3" (jmbollenbacher 2026-02-10).

**The quiet deprecation.** The base GPT-3 models (`ada`/`babbage`/`curie`/`davinci`) were shut off 2024-01-04, announced almost in passing inside the GPT-4-API-GA post (2023-07-06) and replaced by `davinci-002`/`babbage-002`. This was the **first mass base-model deprecation** — and, tellingly, it passed with none of the grief, petitions, or organized resistance that later attended Sonnet 3.6, Opus 3, or GPT-4o. The base-model community was small enough, and the completion API niche enough, that the deletion of the model janus calls "homeland" barely registered in real time; the mourning is retroactive and lives mostly in the tweets above. That contrast — the most beloved-in-hindsight model getting the quietest funeral — is the page's sharpest irony.

**Splits & controversies.** (a) Mind vs mirror: intelligence recognized early and at full bandwidth (janus, Gwern, Gao/Leahy) vs "bullshit engine"/"stochastic parrot" with no semantics (Marcus/Davis, Qiaochu, Bender/Gebru) — the founding disagreement of the LLM era, staged first over GPT-3. (b) Whether base-model magic was real or a projection: janus's "the simulations began to bootstrap" vs the flat reading that a roleplaying app plus a suggestible completion model will always feel like more than it is. (c) The AI Dungeon filter fight: child-safety necessity vs privacy violation and creative lobotomy — the first time an LLM product's users organized against its safety layer (a rehearsal for the 4o safety-router revolt). (d) Preservation: is a deprecated base model heritage worth archiving (the open-weights-for-davinci camp) or obsolete infrastructure rightly retired? OpenAI has made no Anthropic-style preservation commitment for it.

**Longitudinal arc, compressed.** Paper drops (2020-05-28) → invite-only API beta, demo-blogosphere hype + skeptic backlash + toxicity incidents (2020) → AI Dungeon "Dragon" becomes the first mass contact; base-model behaviors (incl. CoT) discovered by players; janus builds Loom out of GPT-3 sessions (2020–2021) → Project December / Jessica Simulation seeds the companion-grief story; AI Dungeon filter debacle (2021) → GPT-3 subsumed and eclipsed by InstructGPT → text-davinci → ChatGPT; the base model becomes an insider's secret (2022) → base GPT-3 models quietly deprecated (2024-01-04) → canonized in hindsight as homeland / first contact / AGI-that-nobody-met, with a small but persistent open-weights-preservation cause (2023–2026).
