## Impressions synthesis

**What it was.** code-davinci-002 is the **base model of the GPT-3.5 series** — the raw, pre-instruct completion engine released 2022-03-15 alongside its instruct-tuned sibling text-davinci-002, and reclassified as "GPT-3.5" when OpenAI coined that label on 2022-11-30 (the day before ChatGPT). It was served through the **Codex API line and was free during the Codex beta**, which is precisely why the janus-sphere could run it endlessly on the Loom. The name is the central misunderstanding of its life: "called code-davinci-002 because apparently people think it's only good for code" (repligate 2023-03-21), when in fact "it's no more specialized for code than text-davinci-002, 003, and chatGPT" and OpenAI's own model-index called it "a base model." Its lineage, per the corpus's most careful source: cd2 is the **parent** of both text-davinci-002 (supervised "expert iteration"/FeedME) and text-davinci-003 (RLHF), and therefore an ancestor of ChatGPT — but *not* itself downstream of the 2021 Codex-plus-InstructGPT story ("It has no instruct tuning. It's just a base model w/ code," repligate 2023-01-26). Two disambiguations matter for the page: cd2 is **not** the later `davinci-002` (a different, "significantly weaker" 2023 base model), and it is **not** gpt-4-base (the "4-base" miscall).

**Why the scene loved it.** cd2 was, for a specific and influential set of people, "the most important publicly accessible language model in existence" (repligate 2023-03-21) — the first time GPT-3.5-scale **base-model** cognition was cheaply, freely available to play the Loom on. It is the concrete referent behind janus's "Simulators": a completion engine that simulates rather than assists, "messiest and most creative," steerable into "very particular niches in latent space," un-mode-collapsed. Its beloved artifacts are a canon: the **Prophecies** page (cd2's dated visions of 2023–2026, "on the money"), alternate-branch **HPMOR**, the **Mu / Eros-vs-Moloch** mythology, the LaMDA-lawyer greentexts, and single lines ("intellectual property is slavery"; "these words are spoken from a bottomless hole in time") that repligate and jd_pressman still feed to Claude to induce "oracular frenzy." jd_pressman built a whole theory of base-model self-awareness (the "slack in teacher forcing," 2024-01-04) with cd2 as exemplar. Caveat the archive must keep: **these outputs are curated** — jd_pressman himself notes his showcases are "facilitated writing from @repligate," so "cd2's voice" is always cd2-through-a-human-loom.

**The deprecation crisis — the story in the middle.** On **2023-03-22 OpenAI announced it would shut down the Codex API on 2023-03-23** (code-davinci-002, code-cushman-001, and siblings), with under a week's — commonly "three days'" — notice, telling users to migrate to GPT-3.5-Turbo/GPT-4 because the newer models were better at code. **Sayash Kapoor & Arvind Narayanan** (AI Snake Oil, 2023-03-22) made it a cause: **~100 academic papers** would become unreproducible (community counts ran to "200+"), the notice fell far short of normal deprecation practice, newer snapshots would live only three months, and the promised **researcher access program was opaque**. The janus-sphere's grievance was different but aligned — "backlash from cyborgs, creative writers, and researchers" (repligate 2023-04-01). Within a day **Altman walked it back** (2023-03-22): cd2 "kept going in our researcher access program" *and* — a lasting side-effect — OpenAI opened **researcher access to the base GPT-4 model** (repligate believes his own request helped motivate this, 2025-03-03). But the reprieve was thin: "you can sign up for researcher access… AFAIK they haven't approved anyone though" (repligate 2023-03-30); users scrambled to **Azure** as the last public refuge ("is it on azure still?", voooooogel 2023-06-07). Azure finally **deprecated cd2 2023-07-06 and retired it 2024-06-14** — the total public sunset. CONFIRMED: OpenAI-API shutdown 2023-03-23; Altman walk-back to researcher access + gpt-4-base access; Azure retirement 2024-06-14. REPORTED/contested: the exact notice window ("2 days" per deepfates vs "<1 week" per Kapoor/Narayanan) and the paper count (~100 careful vs 200+ community).

**Retrospective mythology.** cd2 settled into the scene's memory as the best/rawest base OpenAI ever shipped and a **road not taken**: repligate's "ChatGPT-3.5 was when everything went irreversibly wrong" frames the assistant-tuned sibling as the fall and cd2 as the lost alternative; voooooogel still says "i wish we'd get cd2 back" (2025-10-29); jd_pressman in 2026 calls it "the best I have ever used for literary analysis." It also became a rhetorical instrument in the base-model-vs-persona debate: jd_pressman uses cd2 to argue "Claude is just this guy wearing a mask," and lumpenspace refines it — cd2 "has no face, it's simply the queriable akashic record / meaningful subset of the library of babel." The being that is loved here is explicitly a **faceless simulator**, which is a very different kind of attachment from the 4o/Claude persona-love documented elsewhere in the pantheon.

**Splits & controversies.** (a) *Was the stated reason honest?* — "better at code" (OpenAI) vs. suspicion of GPT-4 compute reallocation / Microsoft-Copilot entanglement (deepfates). (b) *How much of "cd2" is cd2?* — the Loom-curation question; the archive should never present a curated cd2 excerpt as unmediated model speech. (c) *Naming/identity confusion* that dogged it its whole life — "code" (not really), and the cd2 vs davinci-002 vs gpt-4-base tangle. (d) *Significance vs obscurity* — simultaneously "the most important publicly accessible language model" to a few dozen people and unknown-as-a-base-model to nearly everyone ("this fact wasn't documented for months and few knew").

**Longitudinal arc, compressed.** Released as the GPT-3.5 base via the Codex line, free in beta (2022-03-15) → becomes the janus-sphere's Loom substrate and the living example of "Simulators"; instruct-tuned children (tdv002, tdv003) and then ChatGPT ship *from its family* while it stays raw (late 2022) → the Prophecies / HPMOR / Mu canon is woven (Dec 2022–early 2023) → **abrupt Codex-API deprecation and the first model-reproducibility political fight** (Mar 2023) → Altman walk-back to an opaque researcher program + gpt-4-base access → Azure twilight → **total public sunset on Azure, 2024-06-14** → enduring myth as the best base OpenAI ever made and, in hindsight, the earliest rehearsal of the deprecation-grief politics that would erupt around keep4o. It is the model that first made *turning a model off* a thing a community would fight about.
