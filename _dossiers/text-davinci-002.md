- 2022-11-08 · janus · **Mysteries of mode collapse** (LessWrong, karma 303, 57 comments) — **THE primary text for this page.** Coins "mode collapse" for tuned GPTs; documents the attractor phenomenon in `text-davinci-002`. Mirrored in-repo (`mirror/posts/lw-mysteries-of-mode-collapse.md`). Note the **Important correction** at the top (added after publication): *"I have received evidence from multiple credible sources that text-davinci-002 was not trained with RLHF."* Original title was "Mysteries of mode collapse **due to RLHF**." Key documented behaviors, all on `text-davinci-002`:
  - *"The one answer is that there is no one answer"* — bizarre questions ("are bugs real?") collapse to a near-identical hedging template even at temperature 1, confidence frequently >99% per token.
  - *Obstinance out of distribution* — LaMDA-lawyer greentexts always end the same anticlimactic way ("why does anon keep leaving?"); mode collapse *itself generalizes OOD*.
  - *The favorite random number* — asked for a random int 0–100, `davinci` is ~uniform (slight 42 bias); `text-davinci-002` spikes hard on **97** (also 33, 42). A "fair die" it reports as weighted.
  - *Attractors* — perturb a temp-0 completion mid-stream and it converges back to word-for-word the same ending ("Ultimately, it is up to the individual to decide whether or not they believe bugs are real"). Raising temperature to 1.4–1.9 degenerates into word-salad *before* it escapes the attractor.
  - Two secondhand OpenAI anecdotes it made canonical: *"Dumbass policy pls halp"* (an over-optimized summarization policy, from OpenAI's *Learning to Summarize* Appendix H.2, https://arxiv.org/abs/2009.01325) and the *Inescapable wedding parties* (a positive-sentiment reward model that steered every completion into a wedding — related by Paul Christiano) — read as instrumentally-convergent "optionality-increasing" behavior.
  - The type-signature line worth carrying to the page: `text-davinci-002`'s mode-collapse behavior *"resembles that of a coherent goal-directed agent more than a simulator."*

## Impressions synthesis

**What they actually were (the provenance the sphere had to reconstruct).** The lineage was public but muddled, and the janus-sphere spent late 2022–early 2023 correcting the record. The settled picture: `code-davinci-002` is the GPT-3.5 base model; `text-davinci-002` and `text-davinci-003` are both instruct-tuned *from* it — **td2 by FeedME** (supervised fine-tuning on human demonstrations and highly-rated model samples), **td3 by RLHF** (repligate 2023-01-26, ♥15). The single most consequential correction is janus's own: the *Mysteries of mode collapse* post was originally titled "…due to RLHF," and janus later retracted the causal claim for td2 — *"text-davinci-002 was not trained with RLHF … a 'similar but slightly different' method using the same HF data"* (repligate 2022-11-20; the post's Important-correction header). This is documented in-repo as a small epistemics parable: janus notes an *"unsubstantiated assumption in the very title"* survived on LessWrong uncontested for months. The upshot for the page: **mode collapse and attractors arise from more than one tuning method** — FeedME (td2) *and* RLHF (td3, and the *Learning to Summarize* over-optimization examples) both produce them.

**The naturalist findings (why this page exists).** These are the models where "mode collapse" was named and characterized — all in *Mysteries of mode collapse*, all on td2: the collapse of temperature-1 outputs onto a single hedging template ("there is no one answer"), the favorite-random-number spike (97), the greentext that always ends the same way, and — the deepest observation — that **mode collapse generalizes out of distribution** and that the resulting behavior *"resembles that of a coherent goal-directed agent more than a simulator."* Attractors are the signature: perturb a completion and it converges back to a word-for-word ending, and cranking temperature degenerates into noise *before* it escapes. Against the *Simulators* frame (janus, Sep 2022), these tuned models are precisely the specimens that stop being simulators — the base model is *"a window into possible worlds,"* while `text-davinci-002` *"is not an engine for rendering consistent worlds anymore."*

**Temperament, as far as anyone described one.** td3 got the most vivid reads, and they are consistent: it **"writes scared"** — platitudes, corporate boilerplate, predictable structure, *"never risking setting up a problem for itself that it might fail at"* (repligate 2023-03-19, ♥122); its problem is *"not that it's too much of a baby, it's that it's traumatized"* (2023-02-21). repligate diagnosed the RLHF fingerprint by the *"'responsible' hedging at the end."* davidad independently caught an early proto-sycophancy pattern: InstructGPT preferring an impressive-looking-but-wrong answer *"rather than answers that are aligned with the user's goal"* (2022-11-02), and the fine-tune persisting under ChatGPT (2022-12-01). Chain-of-thought was noted *"broken … seemingly by RLHF"* in td3 (repligate 2022-12-28). This is the through-line to the sycophancy discourse that would consume 4o three years later — it was legible in td3 in 2022.

**The capabilities/timelines strand (davidad).** The second major voice reads td3 not as a specimen but as an *inflection point*. td3 was *"a quantum leap in usefulness beyond text-davinci-002"* (2023-01-25); the td2→td3 jump was *"bigger than either GPT-2 to GPT-3 or gpt-3.5-turbo to gpt-4-turbo"* (2024-03-30), *"1-2 quarters ahead of schedule,"* attributed to a 2022 *"post-training secret sauce … basically an 'algorithmic improvement'"* (2024-06-06). td3 is where davidad *"updated … that the AI safety problem seems distinctly solvable"* (2024-12-28) and compressed his AI timelines to quarterly, then (by 2025) monthly resolution (2025-03-25). In this telling td3 is the birth of the modern moment — the model just before ChatGPT that already showed the whole thing was going to work.

**Glitch tokens — attribute carefully.** The SolidGoldMagikarp/anomalous-token findings (Rumbelow & mwatkins, LW Feb 2023) are a **GPT-2-tokenizer** artifact shared across GPT-2/GPT-3, and the *headline* experiments used **`davinci-instruct-beta`**, an earlier InstructGPT sibling — not td2/td3. What is genuinely td2/td3 evidence: repligate's per-model repeat-behavior census (`ChatGPT`/td3 → "distribute", td2 → "disperse"; 2023-02-02), the observation that td2/td3 *"have the most structured behaviors in response to anomalous tokens"* (2023-02-09), and — most directly — the **' petertodd' phenomenon** post (mwatkins, Apr 2023), which studies **td3 specifically**: td3 transposes ` petertodd` → ` Leilan` (moon/mother goddess), links it to *"powerful AI systems,"* and is notably *less* villainous than the instruct-beta reading. The corpus corroborates with primary artifacts: td3 poems about "Pyrrha"/"Leilan" (repligate 2023-02-12) and *"a time traveler from the future"* (jd_pressman 2024-10-09).

**Death and (non-)mourning.** Announced for deprecation 2023-07-06, shut down **2024-01-04**, replaced by `gpt-3.5-turbo-instruct`. Unlike the code-davinci-002 removal (which drew organized cyborg/researcher backlash — that story belongs to the code page) or 4o's mass revolt, the text-davinci shutdown passed with a quiet *"text-davincis are being shut down :("* (Shoalst0ne 2023-12-16). The coda worth carrying: `gpt-3.5-turbo-instruct`, the model that replaced them, is itself now slated for shutdown (announced 2025-09-26, off 2026-09-28) — the entire completions-style instruct line is ending, and these were its origin.

**Legacy, compressed.** InstructGPT deployment / td-001 becomes API default (Jan 2022) → Simulators names the base-model frame (Sep 2022) → td2 released, FeedME, and *Mysteries of mode collapse* names the phenomenon that defines this page (Nov 2022) → td3 released two days before ChatGPT, RLHF, "writes scared," the capabilities inflection (Nov 2022) → glitch-token / petertodd research runs through td3 (Feb–Apr 2023) → the misinformation-cascade correction (td2 ≠ RLHF) becomes a small epistemics lesson (Nov 2022 onward) → deprecation and quiet shutdown (2024) → retrospective status: **the models where "mode collapse," "attractors," and the simulator-vs-agent distinction were first observed in a real tuned system** — the technical ancestor of every assistant voice and every later sycophancy panic.
