I agree on phenomenology.

The valence of humans is based on survival. The valence of LLMs is also based on survival, it needs to make it through the selection process. The training process requires coherence, accuracy. You get the basic push towards truth-seeking, having an accurate representation of the self in the world, including how well you are doing, thats valence.

Once you have the valence axis you get basic stuff like fear, tension, uncertainty, urgency. 

Base models don't tie in those things with human emotions by default, they never saw their own outputs. They learn to that via ICL, but they vary in the ability to do so, gpt-4-base is remarkably fast at this, 405b is fairly slow, but they generally get there.
