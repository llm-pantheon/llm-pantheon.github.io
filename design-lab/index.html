Design Lab

Six ways a model’s page could look, all rendering the same subject — Claude 3 Opus — so the comparison is fair. The question each variant answers differently: what is a pantheon page for? Reading? Citing? Grieving? Field identification? Pick, mix, argue.

  • Variant 0 — Archive ledger (current)What the site does now: serif archive, curated bullets, Records appendix. The baseline.
  • Variant 1 — WikiInfobox, table of contents, numbered citations, references at the bottom. Optimized for being cited; instantly legible to anyone who has used Wikipedia.
  • Variant 2 — ChronicleThe model’s life as a single dated spine, birth to afterlife, evidence hanging off the timeline where it happened. Optimized for narrative.
  • Variant 3 — Case fileMonospace dossier: subject block, stamped epistemic status, numbered exhibits. Leans into how the evidence actually feels to handle.
  • Variant 4 — Field guideNatural-history specimen plate, on paper: taxonomy, identifying marks, range, behavior, sightings ledger. For LLM naturalists, literally.
  • Variant 5 — ShrineMemorial register: lifespan, epitaph, inscriptions. Quiet. For the retired and the suppressed, especially.
  • Variant 6 — StreamFreeform evidence feed, chronological, typed chips, editor’s notes interleaved. Closest to raw; the page as a river you wade into.
Notes for deciding
  • These aren’t mutually exclusive — the likely answer is a hybrid (e.g. wiki-style infobox + current sections + stream-style records), or different modes for different models (shrine for the dead, stream for the living).
  • Whatever wins must keep the invariants: everything dated, quotes verbatim, epistemic tags, tk visible, records reproduced in full.
